## Abstract 

Integrating frame-based RGB cameras with event streams offers a promising solution for robust ob-ject detection under challenging dynamic condi-tions. However, the inherent heterogeneity and data redundancy of these modalities often lead to prohibitive computational overhead or suboptimal feature fusion. In this paper, we propose Hyper-FEOD, a high-performance and efficient detection framework, which synergistically optimizes multi-modal interaction through two core components. First, we introduce Sparse Hypergraph-enhanced Cross-Modal Fusion (S-HCF), which leverages the inherent sparsity of event streams to con-struct an event-guided activity map. By perform-ing high-order hypergraph modeling exclusively on selected motion-critical sparse tokens, S-HCF captures complex non-local dependencies between RGB and event data while overcoming the tradi-tional complexity bottlenecks of hypergraph com-putation. Second, we design a Fine-Grained Mix-ture of Experts (FG-MoE) Enhancement module to address the diverse semantic requirements of differ-ent image regions. This module employs special-ized hypergraph experts tailored for object bound-aries, internal textures, and backgrounds, utiliz-ing a pixel-level spatial gating mechanism to adap-tively route and enhance features. Combined with a load-balancing loss and zero-initialization strategy, FG-MoE ensures stable training and precise fea-ture refinement without disrupting the pre-trained backbone’s distribution. Experimental results on mainstream RGB-Event benchmarks demonstrate that Hyper-FEOD achieves a superior accuracy-efficiency trade-off, outperforming state-of-the-art methods while maintaining a lightweight footprint suitable for real-time edge deployment.

## 2 Related Work 

2.1 Frame-Event Object Detection 

The inherent complementary properties between frame and event data have driven the rapid advancement of frame-event object detection research. Early works employ late fusion strategies to fuse multi-modal data, which yields relatively low fusion efficiency. For instance, the work in [Chen, 2018] leverages non-maximum suppression to integrate the object detection outputs of the two modalities. Recent works focus on mid-level fusion paradigms, which typically em-ploy attention mechanisms to conduct cross-modal interac-tion and fusion for improved detection performance [Gehrig and Scaramuzza, 2024; Zhou et al. , 2023; Li et al. , 2023; Cao et al. , 2024a; Zhang et al. , 2024]. SODFormer [Li et al. , 2023] integrates events and frames via spatiotemporal transformer to continuously detect objects in an asynchronous manner. CAFR [Cao et al. , 2024a] design bidirectional cross-modality interaction to facilitate the adaptive feature fusion in coarse-to-fine way. Unlike previous studies, this paper har-nesses hypergraph computation to enhance intra- and inter-modality high-order information interaction, thus further ex-panding the performance boundary of multi-modal fusion. 

2.2 Visual Hypergraph Computation 

Hypergraph is the extension of graph. A hypergraph G can be defined by the hyperedge set E and the vertex set V, i.e. , G =

{E , V} . Different from traditional graphs, each hyperedge of a hypergraph can connect multiple vertices, and thus complex correlations between multiple vertices can be modeled. Hy-pergraph learning can facilitate the correlation-guided anal-ysis by capturing complex and higher-order correlations in data based on hypergraph structures. Gao [Gao et al. , 2023] propose a higher-order message spatial propagation method between vertices, which further enhance the capability of hy-pergraph learning. Recently, several works [Han et al. , 2023; Lei et al. , 2025b; Fixelle, 2025] have applied hypergraph computation to computer vision. HyperYOLO [Feng et al. ,2024] is the first work to apply hypergraph computation to object detection, proposing a semantic collecting and scat-tering framework and modeling hyperedges as ϵ-ball cen-tered at each feature point. ## 3 Method 

In this section, we introduce our proposed Hyper-FEOD, a sparse hypergraph- and fine-grained MoE-enhanced efficient framework for FEOD task. The overview of our proposed method is first introduced in Sec. 3.1. Then, Sec. 3.2 and Sec. 3.3 introduces the S-HCF and FG-MoE modules, respec-tively. Finally, we give the overall loss in Sec. 3.4. 

3.1 Overview 

Fig. 2 depicts the overall pipeline of our proposed Hyper-FEOD, which takes event streams and RGB frames as in-puts, and then uses a two-stream encoder to extract frame and event features. Subsequently, our method deploys the Sparse Hypergraph-enhanced Cross-Modal Fusion (S-HCF) mod-ule to simultaneously strengthen intra-modality and inter-modality high-order information interactions between cross-modal features, while significantly reducing computational overhead by focusing only on motion-critical sparse tokens. Specifically, S-HCF leverages event-guided sparse hyper-graph construction and adaptive hypergraph attention to en-able efficient non-local high-order information flow. Next, Hyper-FEOD leverages addition and PAFPN [Liu et al. ,2018] to realize deep integration of multi-modal features. To further enhance the effective optimization process of cross-modal fusion, Hyper-FEOD incorporates the Fine-Grained Mixture of Experts (FG-MoE) Enhancement module, which employs region-specialized hypergraph experts and pixel-level spatial gating to adaptively refine object boundaries, in-ternal textures and background features. Specifically, FG-MoE utilizes a load-balancing loss and zero-initialization strategy to ensure stable training and precise feature refine-ment without disrupting the backbone distribution. Notably, the expert gating and auxiliary loss are only integrated during the model training phase and introduce no additional over-head to inference. 

3.2 Sparse Hypergraph-enhanced Cross-Modal Fusion 

Recent research has shown that attention-driven cross-modal feature interaction can substantially boost the performance of multi-modal fusion architectures. Nevertheless, the at-tention mechanism [Vaswani et al. , 2017] is fundamentally equivalent to modeling pairwise pixel-level correlations on a fully connected semantic graph, an inherent property that restricts it to capturing solely pairwise correlations. Our proposed HIFI leverages hypergraph frameworks to facili-tate global multi-to-multi high-order information interaction among high-level cross-modal features, mitigating this in-trinsic constraint. Hypergraphs constitute a generalization of conventional graphs and are formally defined by a hyperedge set E and the vertex set V, i.e. , G = {E , V} . In contrast to con-ventional graph edges, which are constrained to connecting exactly two vertices, a hyperedge can link multiple vertices through a single association. Thus, hypergraph could model high-order correlations between multiple vertices. As illus-trated in Fig. 3, HIFI employs the Cross-modal Hyperedges Construct (CHC) module to generate global modality-shared hyperedges, and designs the Adaptive Hypergraph Attention (AHA) module to simultaneously implement intra- and inter-modality high-order feature interaction. In the following sub-sections, we provide a detailed elaboration of the CHC and AHA modules, respectively. 

Cross-modal Hyperedges Construct

This strategy ef-fectively preserves the spatial structural information of the original feature maps, making it highly suitable for visual hypergraph computation tasks. Datasets. We conduct experiments on three existing large-scale FEOD datasets, i.e. , DSEC dataset [Gehrig and Scara-muzza, 2024], NightDVS22 [Jiang et al. , 2023] , and PKU-DAVIS-SOD dataset [Li et al. , 2023]. • DSEC dataset [Gehrig and Scaramuzza, 2024] is a driving-centric dataset featuring two event cameras (640 × 480 pixels) and two RGB cameras ( 1440 × 1080 

pixels). It provides 60 sequences (70379 frames, 390118 bounding boxes) across 8 object classes: Pedestrians, Riders, Cars, Buses, Trucks, Bicycles, and Motorcycles. • NightDVS22 dataset [Jiang et al. , 2023] is a multi-modal object detection benchmark acquired with a spa-tial resolution of 346 × 260 pixels. It consists of 533 training scenes and 59 test scenes, encompassing a total of 49,680 RGB frames with high-frequency annotations covering the vehicle and person categories. • PKU-DAVIS-SOD dataset [Li et al. , 2023] is a large-scale event-based detection dataset, which simultane-ously records event streams and RGB frames. The dataset contains 220 sequences, comprising over 1.08 million bounding boxes, and covering three categories, 

i.e. , Cars, Pedestrians, and Two-Wheelers.

The Impact of Fine-grained MoE . Table 3 presents the quantitative results of the ablation experiments of FG-MoE module. We utilize variants of the model with and without distillation on frame and event branch. It can be clearly ob-served that performing self-distillation on either the frame or event branch individually yields performance improvements for the model, yet the gain margin is relatively modest. In detail, Frame Distillation only leads to a 1.4 mAP improve-ment, and Event Distillation results in a 1.7 mAP increase. Only when Frame Distillation and Event Distillation are ap-plied simultaneously does the model achieve a substantial performance boost, with the gain far exceeding that of single-branch distillation. This phenomenon can be attributed to the fact that single-branch self-distillation induces a modal feature imbalance, where the distilled branch dominates the cross-modal feature interaction and fusion process due to its enhanced representational capacity. In contrast, bilateral dis-tillation effectively balances the representation learning of multi-modal features, alleviating the modal dominance issue and thus enabling a more efficient and stable optimization process for cross-modal fusion.

## 5 Conclusion 

This paper presents Hyper-FEOD, a sparse hypergraph- and fine-grained MoE-enhanced efficient detection framework tailored for the FEOD task. Hyper-FEOD enhances cross-modal fusion performance by optimizing sparse high-order interaction and region-aware feature refinement. Specifi-cally, S-HCF enables efficient non-local high-order informa-tion interaction via event-guided sparse hypergraph attention, thus overcoming the limitation of prior methods restricted to dense pairwise correlation modeling. For adaptive feature en-hancement, we leverage specialized hypergraph experts and pixel-level spatial gating to refine object boundaries, tex-tures and backgrounds, yielding more effective cross-modal fusion with lightweight computation and no extra inference overhead. Extensive experiments verify the effectiveness and SOTA performance of our proposed Hyper-FEOD. Future re-search will focus on optimizing the sparse hypergraph mecha-nism for temporal high-order modeling, aiming to effectively capture spatiotemporal correlations in dynamic frame-event data.
